# Burr Grinder (GR-100) Troubleshooting

## Grinder won't turn on
- Confirm the hopper and grounds bin are both seated fully. The GR-100 has a
  safety interlock and will not run unless both are locked in place.
- Check that the outlet has power. Try a different outlet.
- If the motor hums but the burrs don't turn, beans may be jammed. Unplug,
  remove the hopper, and clear any beans lodged in the burr chamber.

## Grind is too fine or too coarse
Turn the grind-adjustment ring on the hopper. Lower numbers are finer (espresso),
higher numbers are coarser (French press). Adjust only while the grinder is
running to avoid stressing the burrs.

## Grinder is loud or vibrating
Some noise is normal. Excessive rattling usually means the hopper isn't seated.
A grinding/metal sound means beans may contain a foreign object (a stone) —
stop immediately and inspect.

## Cleaning
Run the brush through the burr chamber weekly. Do not wash burrs with water;
they will rust. Wipe the hopper with a dry cloth. The grounds bin is
hand-wash only.

## Warranty
The GR-100 carries a 2-year warranty covering motor and burr defects. Wear from
normal use (dulled burrs after heavy use) is not covered.
